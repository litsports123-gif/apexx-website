Whole Wheat FlourGrain
✅ Generally Safe
▾
What is it?
Ground wheat grain with the bran and germ intact. The least processed form of wheat flour, retaining fiber and nutrients that white flour loses during processing.
Why it matters for you
A whole food ingredient with a well-established nutritional profile. Provides dietary fiber supporting gut health, B vitamins for energy metabolism, and minerals like magnesium. Note: not suitable for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.
Soybean OilVegetable Oil
⚠️ Moderate Concern
▾
What is it?
Oil extracted from soybeans. One of the most widely used cooking oils in processed foods due to its low cost and high smoke point. Over 90% of US soybean crops are genetically modified.
Why it matters for you
Contains very high levels of omega-6 polyunsaturated fats. The modern Western diet already has far too much omega-6 relative to omega-3, which multiple studies link to chronic inflammation. Worth being mindful of your total consumption across products.
Better alternatives
✓ Look for: Avocado oil · Extra virgin olive oil · Coconut oil
TBHQPreservative
🚫 Avoid This
▾
What is it?
Tertiary Butylhydroquinone — a synthetic antioxidant preservative derived from petroleum. Used to extend the shelf life of fats and oils. Banned in Japan and restricted in Europe.
Why it matters for you
Animal studies have shown TBHQ causes precancerous stomach tumors at higher doses. A 2020 study linked it to immune system disruption in humans. It appears in dozens of products people eat daily, creating cumulative exposure that's not well studied.
Better alternatives
✓ Look for: Rosemary extract · Vitamin E (tocopherols) · No added preservatives
Natural FlavorsFlavoring Agent
⚠️ Moderate Concern
▾
What is it?
A catch-all term that legally allows manufacturers to use any of over 2,800 approved flavoring substances without disclosing them specifically on the label.
Why it matters for you
The term is deliberately vague. Because manufacturers don't have to disclose what's inside "natural flavors," people with allergies or dietary restrictions (halal, kosher, vegan) have no way of knowing what they're consuming. PureLabel flags this so you can make an informed decision.
